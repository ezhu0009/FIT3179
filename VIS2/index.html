Where are these Attacks?

The chloropeth map of Australian States shows New South Wales having the most incidents recorded, 447 attacks, likely due to hotspots or regional coasts with victims in unpatrolled areas. Following NSW, Queensland follows with 372 attacks.

The state/territory with the least number of incidents recorded is the Northern Territory, with only 19 attacks. This could be explained by the lack of ocean and contact with sharks on its coastal regions.
